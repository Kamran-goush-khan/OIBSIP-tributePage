Early Life and Education

  • 1) Khan was born in Rajasthan to a Muslim family of Pathan ancestry. Khan's mother, Saeeda Begum Khan, and father, Yaseen Ali Khan, were from the Khajuriya village near Rajasthan's Tonk district, and ran a tyre business.
  • 2) Irrfan was good at cricket and was selected to play in the CK Nayudu Trophy for emerging players in the under-23 category, a tournament seen as a stepping stone to first-class cricket in India.
  • 3) Khan completed his MA in Jaipur before joining National School of Drama (NSD) in New Delhi in 1984 to study acting.

Success

  • 1) Irrfan Khan won a Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture for his role as a police inspector in the 2008 film Slumdog Millionaire.
  • 2) Khan acted in several notable films, including Life of Pi, for which he received critical acclaim and won the National Film Award for Best Actor for his role in Paan Singh Tomar.
  • 3) Khan also acted in successful Hindi films like Hindi Medium, which earned him the Filmfare Award for Best Actor, and his final film appearance was in Angrezi Medium, which was released in March 2020.

National Recognition

  • 1) His first Bollywood lead role came in 2005 with film Rog. His performance was praised by critics; one wrote, "Irfaan's eyes speak louder than his words and every time he is in frame, be it talking to his buddy Manish or arguing with Suhel, he shows his capability as an actor"
  • 2) In 2004, he won the Filmfare Best Villain Award for his role in film Haasil. In 2007, he appeared in the box office hits Life in a... Metro, for which he received the Filmfare Best Supporting Actor Award

Personal life

  • 1) Irrfan Khan, who graduated from the National School of Drama (NSD), married fellow NSD graduate Sutapa Sikdar in 1995, and they had two sons. In 2012, he changed the spelling of his first name from "Irfan" to "Irrfan" because he liked the sound of the extra "r," and later dropped his surname "Khan" because he wanted his work to define him, according to a 2016 interview.

Illness and death

  • Irrfan Khan was diagnosed with neuroendocrine cancer in 2018 and received treatment in the UK for a year before returning to India. He was admitted to the hospital in April 2020 due to a colon infection caused by the disease and passed away at the age of 53. His mother had also passed away just four days prior. His death was mourned by people in the film industry and literary figures worldwide, and he was buried at the Versova Kabristan in Mumbai.
